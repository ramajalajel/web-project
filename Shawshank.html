The Shawshank Redemption

Writer:
Frank Darabont — wrote the screenplay and directed the film, based on a short story titled Rita Hayworth and Shawshank Redemption by Stephen King.

Director: Frank Darabont
Year: 1994
Rating: ⭐ 9.3/10 on IMDb — the highest-rated film in IMDb history.

Plot Summary:
The Shawshank Redemption is an American drama film that tells the story of Andy Dufresne, a successful banker who is wrongfully imprisoned for the murder of his wife and her lover. Inside the harsh Shawshank prison, Andy forms a deep friendship with fellow inmate Red, and gradually finds hope and meaning in life despite the brutal conditions.

The film addresses themes of hope, freedom, justice, and the power of holding on to hope even in the darkest times. It is known for its powerful quotes, moving soundtrack, and outstanding performances by Tim Robbins (Andy) and Morgan Freeman (Red).

Nominations:
The film was nominated for 7 Academy Awards, including:
- Best Picture
- Best Supporting Actor (Morgan Freeman)
- Best Adapted Screenplay
Though it did not win, it became one of the most beloved and iconic films in cinema history.
